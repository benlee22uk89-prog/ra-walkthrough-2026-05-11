The thesis

One pattern, one family of solutions.

Senior people are spending their most valuable time on the cheapest part of the work.

Briefing notes, event briefs, occupier pitches, residential briefs, award entries — five expressions of one pattern.

The problem
Senior people on low-value assembly. Gathering, formatting, and structuring information AI should be handling first.
The cause
The same information assembled repeatedly, with no reusable formats for brand context, briefing structures, or event templates.
The solution
Templates, source material, and brand voice combined into a structured first draft your team reviews and improves.
Start with a single pilot. Save time in the first week — scale it. If not, change it or move on.

    Bi-weekly retainer check-in
    Forty-five minutes, with an agenda shared ahead and notes shared the same day.

    Every Friday. What we did, what's planned, what's blocked, and what we'd like from you.

    Email or Teams — we'd come back within a working day.

    Pre-read sent five working days ahead. Follow-up materials and recording within 48 hours.

    Defining what good looks like

    Agreed together. In writing. Before we start.

    1. 01
      A short workshop in the first week
      About forty-five minutes — you, Jenny, Mabel and Ben — to talk through what each workstream needs to achieve, what would tell us it's working, and what would tell us it isn't.
    2. 02
      Captured in writing
      A single Success Criteria document with the criteria, the signals we'd watch for, and the review cadence. Yours to sign off.
    3. 03
      Sign-off before delivery starts
      Nothing starts until you've signed off. We don't define success on our own, and we don't define it after the fact.
    4. 04
      Reviewed at every pilot checkpoint
      A brief check at the end of each training session, plus a formal review at month three. We'd look at every active pilot and decide together — scale it, refine it, or shelve it.
    Set right at the start. Reviewed against the same standard throughout. No ambiguity at the end.
